# Kitchen Reset Intention

## Purpose

This project defines a simple solo workflow for resetting the kitchen room to a calm, joyful baseline in 10 minutes.

The intention is not to deep-clean the room or finish every dish immediately. The intention is to reduce overwhelm with a low-mental-load routine that restores the room to a state that feels usable, cared for, and good to be in.

## Desired End State

At the end of the reset:

- Dishes are reset and staged for later.
- Work surfaces are clear and usable.
- The floor has been swept.
- The couch has been reset.
- The heater has been reset.
- The room feels calm, orderly, and joyful to re-enter.

## Workflow Philosophy

This reset is designed around low mental load, low friction, and a fixed 10-minute time box.

- Focus on actions that reduce overwhelm quickly.
- Clear and contain rather than deep-clean.
- Stage dishes for later instead of requiring full completion now.
- Prioritize visible order and ease of re-entry into the room.
- Keep the routine simple enough to follow when already overwhelmed.

## Operational Definition Of "Reset"

A kitchen reset means the room has returned to a stable, joyful in-between state:

- dishes are contained and staged for later,
- work surfaces are usable again,
- the floor, couch, and heater have been restored to order,
- the room feels calm enough to enter without added stress.

## Current Physical Workflow

1. Reset the dishes by putting away what is ready and staging the rest for later.
2. Clear the work surfaces so the room becomes usable again.
3. Reset the couch.
4. Sweep the floor.
5. Reset the heater.
6. Finish once the room feels calm, functional, and no longer overwhelming.

## What This Is Not

This routine is not deep cleaning.

- It does not require fully washing every dish.
- It does not require intensive scrubbing or detailed cleaning.
- It is a reset to baseline, not a full restoration project.
